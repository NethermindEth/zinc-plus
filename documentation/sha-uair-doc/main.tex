\begin{abstract}
We describe the Universal AIR with lookups (UAIR$^+$) instance that
arithmetizes $N$ chained SHA-256 compression invocations, as deployed
in the Zinc+ reference implementation. We follow the conventions and
notation of the main Zinc+ paper, and focus on the design choices
that depart from the idealized arithmetization presented there:
namely, \emph{(i)}~ chained-compression packing into a single
$2^{\nu}$-row trace; \emph{(ii)}~ replacement of the Table~9
materialization columns by virtual binary-polynomial residuals pinned
through booleanity; \emph{(iii)}~ packing of the five integer-carry
columns into a single binary-polynomial column;
\emph{(iv)}~ an all-$\Q[X]$ instantiation that uses public
\emph{compensator} columns to keep the SHA recurrence constraints at
algebraic degree~$1$ in the trace MLEs.
\end{abstract}
