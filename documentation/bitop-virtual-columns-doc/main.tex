\begin{abstract}
We describe a virtual-column construction in the Zinc+ multilinear PIOP
that allows constraints to reference bit-wise rotations and right-shifts
of binary-polynomial trace columns at zero marginal cost: no fresh
commitment, no additional protocol round, no extra challenge, and only
a single field element of communication per declared virtual column.
The construction rests on a structural commutation lemma: the
multilinear extension functor over the row coordinate commutes with
any $\F_2$-linear endomorphism of the cell ring acting on the
coefficient coordinate. This commutation is exploited as a
\emph{verifier-side reconstruction}: at the final challenge point, the
verifier reproduces the value of the virtual MLE locally from the
opened lifted evaluation of its source column. We formalise the
construction, sketch its soundness, exhibit its communication-cost
profile against the naive commit-and-equate approach, and observe that
the same template lifts verbatim to any $\F_2$-linear function of the
$32$ coefficient bits of a cell.
\end{abstract}
