\begin{abstract}
We describe the Universal AIR with lookups (UAIR$^+$) instance that
arithmetizes ECDSA signature verification on the $\secp$ elliptic
curve, as deployed in the Zinc+ reference implementation. We follow
the conventions and notation of the main Zinc+ paper, and focus on
the design choices made by the implementation: namely,
\emph{(i)}~native arithmetization in the $\F_p$ branch (no $\F_2[X]$
or bit-polynomial columns), where every cell is \emph{stored} as a
$320$-bit integer (\texttt{Int<5>}) carrying the canonical
representative of an $\F_p$ value, and constraints are checked as
$(p)$-ideal-membership identities over $\Z$ — the proving field $F$
is a separate small random prime field, not $\F_p$ itself
(\Cref{sec:cols});
\emph{(ii)}~Shamir's trick to merge the two scalar multiplications
into a single $256$-row double-and-add loop;
\emph{(iii)}~Jacobian coordinates throughout the loop, with the
field inversion required for the affine readout deferred off-protocol
and, in the deployed UAIR, \emph{no in-circuit binding of
$R_x \equiv r \pmod n$} (\Cref{rem:final-readout});
\emph{(iv)}~per-row addend coordinates supplied by the verifier
(rather than committed and bound in-circuit), reducing the witness to
$8$ field-element columns and the constraint count to $11$.
\end{abstract}
